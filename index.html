The Sensory Promise

Never startled awake again.

Traditional alarms are designed to shock you conscious. For autistic and ADHD brains, that shock can trigger fight-or-flight responses, sensory overload, and morning meltdowns before the day even begins. NeuralWake™ takes a different approach.

🛡️

Sensory Guardian

Ultra-minimal stimulation. Starts at 30 dB, never exceeds 60 dB. Simple tones between 200–500 Hz. Warm amber light only. The "never startle" engineering guarantee.

🌉

Sensory Bridge

Multimodal but gentle. Layers light, vibration, and sound at low intensity — combined for synergy without overwhelming any single sense.

⚡

Deep Activation

For deep sleepers who need stronger input. Higher volume, brighter visuals, faster ramp — but still capped at 80 dB. Melodic sounds only, never flat beeps.

📳

Haptic-Only Mode

Skip audio entirely, wake by vibration alone. For the 50–70% of autistic individuals who experience decreased sound tolerance (hyperacusis).1

🌅

Dawn Simulation

Screen-based pre-alarm light ramp starting 15–60 minutes before your alarm. OLED-optimized warm amber progression — because your brain needs a sunrise, not a siren.

🔇

80 dB Hardware Cap

63% of autistic individuals cannot tolerate stimulation above 80 dB.3 NeuralWake enforces this as an absolute ceiling across all profiles. No exceptions.

The Science

Guided by neuroscience, not marketing buzzwords.

Other alarm apps say "science-backed" without citing a single study. NeuralWake™ is grounded in two comprehensive research reports covering 16 research questions with citations to published, peer-reviewed literature. Every design decision traces to real research.

🧠

ADHD sleep inertia lasts 45–120+ minutes

Driven by delayed circadian phase (73–80% are evening chronotypes),4 low basal morning cortisol, and prefrontal cortex hypoactivation that's 30–40% behind typical development.5 NeuralWake's phased arousal system starts simple and escalates gradually — meeting the brain where it is.

Kooij & Van Veen, 2010 · Barkley, 1997 · Li Fulun et al., 2025

🌙

Autistic brains have no biological dawn signal

Increased slow-wave sleep (43.8% vs. 35.9% controls)6 creates deeper "sleep wells." Flat melatonin curves mean no natural signal to transition from sleep to wakefulness.7 NeuralWake's dawn simulation provides the missing sunrise.

Kawai et al., 2022 · Rossignol & Frye, 2011

🔊

Autistic brains do not habituate to aversive sound

Unlike ADHD brains, where alarm sounds fade into background noise over time, autistic brains experience alarm sounds as more aversive with repetition — not less. 63% cannot tolerate stimulation above 80 dB.3 This is why the hardware cap and sensory profiles exist.

Khalfa et al., 2004 · Takahashi et al., 2014

⏰

Clock tasks are an autistic cognitive strength

Three independent neuroscience frameworks — hyper-systemizing theory (Baron-Cohen),8 Enhanced Perceptual Functioning (Mottron),9 and Weak Central Coherence (Happé & Frith)10 — converge to predict that rule-based, detail-oriented clock tasks are optimal for autistic cognition. This is why Fix the Clock is the recommended default for ASD profiles.

Baron-Cohen, 2006 · Mottron, 2006 · Happé & Frith, 2006

🎯

Positive reinforcement approximates stimulant medication

For ADHD brains, positive reinforcement improves attention at rates comparable to stimulant medication.11 Punishment is highly ineffective for ADHD habit formation. This is why NeuralWake reframes snoozing as "Bonus Round!" and uses streak shields instead of streak-breaking.

Hawk et al., 2014 · Sundelin et al., 2024

My Story

Built by an autistic developer, for brains like ours.

NeuralWake started from a simple question: "What annoys you lately?"

I was trying to brainstorm a coding project but didn't have any real ideas. So I asked Claude to help me think of something. Instead of suggesting projects, it asked me about my actual daily friction points — the small, personal, "weird" frustrations. My immediate thought: I hate my alarm in the morning.

I thought back to the pre-smartphone era, when I used to purposely set my radio alarm clock to the wrong time. Every morning, I'd have to calculate in my head what time it actually was. I instinctively knew that mental calculation helped me feel awake faster. I had to keep changing the clock display so it didn't become routine math.

Back then, I didn't know I'm autistic — I wasn't diagnosed until my early 30s. When I got that diagnosis, my entire past finally made sense. I was always a little odd, didn't understand social norms, and never understood why. Once I had my diagnosis, everything clicked.

I also didn't realize my little radio alarm clock trick was grounded in real neuroscience. When I started reading the research for this project — over 40 papers on sleep inertia, ADHD dopamine systems, autistic sensory processing, circadian rhythms — I discovered that three independent neuroscience frameworks converge to predict that clock-reading tasks are optimal for autistic cognition. My pre-diagnosis intuition was backed by science I didn't know existed.

That same research revealed something else: traditional alarms actively work against neurodivergent biology. They trigger fight-or-flight responses that can derail an entire day before it starts. And the apps that claim to help? They show you ads when you're half-conscious, harvest your health data, and charge you $60 a year for the privilege.

NeuralWake is what I built instead. An alarm that works with your brain, not against it. Designed from peer-reviewed neuroscience research for the brains that need it most. 100% free forever — because the "ADHD tax" of expensive subscriptions targeting people who struggle with cancellation is a real problem, and charging for something this essential felt wrong.

NeuralWake is my first real app. I hope you enjoy using it. And if you like it enough to tell a friend, a family member, or a co-worker about it — that would truly mean everything to me.
